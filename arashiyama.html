Arashiyama Bamboo Forest is located in the densely populated Kyoto area. The massive evergreen bamboo plants tower up to 92 ft, but the origin of the forest is an area of debate. One suggestion is it was planted to protect the nearby Tenryu-ji temple.

Japan's Ministry of the Environment considers the rustling sounds of the bamboo one of the essential soundscapes of Japan

The Moso Bamboo

Originating from China and Taiwan, the Moso Bamboo is a species of giant bamboo that can be found in many areas south of Hokkaido. It can reach nearly 100 feet in height.
