Shirakami-Sanchi (白神山地) is home to the last virgin forest of Siebold’s beech, a tree that once covered most of northern Japan. As it is a protected World Heritage Site, entering the heart of the forest requires permission from forest management. It is a remnant of the cool-temperate beech forests that covered Japan up to twelve thousand years ago.

The Seibold Beech

Seibold’s beech trees are the dominant trees of Japan's deciduous forests and grow up to 116 feet in height. Because beech trees are unsuitable for cultivation of Shitake mushrooms, they are largely undisturbed in the vast area of the Shirakami-Sanchi

The Monkey Who Loves Snow

Shirakami-Sanchi is home to the Japanese macaque, or snow monkey, which is a species native to Japan. Their snow monkey moniker derives from their living in snow covered areas, and no other primate lives as far north as this macaque.

The macaque is especially memorable for its love of hot spring baths. They also exhibit unusual behavior in their enjoyment of rolling snowballs.
